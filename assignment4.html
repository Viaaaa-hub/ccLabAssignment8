Assignment 4

Reflection

Partners’ names： Lamisse Hamouda, Manahil Karim This week’s assignment was done in a group. Each of us designed a character with a head, body, and legs, then chose one part from each of the other two members’ designs to combine with selected parts of our own and create a new character. We all kept the heads from our own designs. I used Manahil’s design of body and Lamisse’s design of legs. Manahil designed a cute yellow guy with a party hat, Lamisse designed a gentel man in black suits. I designed a tomato girl in dress. Reflection： In this week’s assignment, I deepened my understanding of how to use functions, as well as arguments and parameters. With this approach, when I want to draw multiple identical shapes, I no longer need to repeat large blocks of code; I can simply call the function multiple times and change the parameters. This is very convenient and makes my code much cleaner. In addition, I ran into an interesting issue: because the body and head overlap, the body was covering the head. I quickly realized this was due to the call order inside draw()—I was calling the body function before the head, which produced an unexpected layer (z-order). After reordering the function calls, the problem was solved.
